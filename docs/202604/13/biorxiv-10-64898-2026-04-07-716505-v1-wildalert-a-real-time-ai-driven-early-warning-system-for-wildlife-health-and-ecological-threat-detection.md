## 摘要
新兴传染病和环境扰动正日益威胁野生动物与生态系统的健康。自由活动的野生动物常常作为生态系统不稳定的早期指标，因此及时检测发病与死亡事件对早期预警至关重要。然而，现有系统缺乏实时暴发检测的分析能力。我们提出 WildAlert，这是一种由人工智能驱动的早期预警系统，将经过微调的基于 BERT 的自然语言处理模型与无监督异常检测框架相结合，用于利用来自野生动物救护机构的实时临床前诊断数据识别异常的野生动物健康事件。NLP 模块在临床分类与入院情况识别方面表现出高准确率，从而实现了临床前诊断的综合症监测框架。回顾性验证显示，WildAlert 识别的异常通常与已确诊的健康事件同时出现或先于其发生，这些事件包括高致病性禽流感（HPAI）、有害藻华相关中毒、海龟低温昏迷、大规模搁浅事件、西尼罗病毒感染及支原体病（mycoplasmosis）。WildAlert 建立了全球最大、标准化、近实时的野生动物健康监测系统，将野生动物救护临床记录转化为可执行情报，能够在多个类群和区域中检测异常，且往往早于其他监测方法。WildAlert 提供了可迁移的分析框架和可扩展的 One Health 模型，将生物多样性监测、人畜共患病应急准备以及与生态系统相关的环境威胁联系起来，对保护、生物安全和环境风险应对具有重要意义。

## Abstract
Emerging infections and environmental disruptions increasingly threaten wildlife and ecosystem health. Free-ranging wildlife often serve as early indicators of ecological instability, making timely detection of morbidity and mortality events critical for early warning. Yet, existing systems lack the analytical capacity for real-time outbreak detection. We present WildAlert, an AI-driven early warning system that integrates fine-tuned BERT-based natural language processing models with an unsupervised anomaly detection framework to identify unusual wildlife health events using real-time pre-diagnostic clinical data from wildlife rehabilitation organizations. The NLP module achieved high accuracy across clinical classifications and circumstances of admissions, enabling a pre-diagnostic syndromic surveillance framework. Retrospective validation demonstrated that WildAlert anomalies frequently coincided with or preceded confirmed morbidity events, including highly pathogenic avian influenza (HPAI), harmful algal bloom-associated toxicosis, cold-stunning in sea turtles, mass stranding events, West Nile virus, and mycoplasmosis. WildAlert establishes the world's largest standardized, near real-time wildlife health surveillance system, transforming wildlife rehabilitation clinical records into actionable intelligence capable of detecting anomalies across taxa and regions, often before other surveillance methods. WildAlert provides a transferable analytical framework and scalable One Health model linking biodiversity monitoring, zoonotic disease preparedness, and ecosystem-linked environmental threats, with implications for conservation, public health and environmental hazard response.
